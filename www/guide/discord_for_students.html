Consultation will also be via Discord.

Getting Started

The first thing you will see after clicking the link above is the Server Rules. Be sure to read this carefully, as this is your contract for using Discord in this Unit. Students who fail to follow the rules may face disciplinary action.

Your Name is Important

Rule number 1 is important, and tells you to change your nickname for the server so we know who you are! You can do this by finding your account on the right-hand-side of the Discord interface, right-clicking and choosing Profile. In the popup, set your name to match the following format:

First Name (username)
e.g. Lindsay (lfwells)

You may also choose to include your preferred pronouns or use your preferred (English) name.

After accepting the rules and setting your name, explore the channels on the left-hand side of the screen. Why not #introduce-yourself and start chatting in #off-topic to get the ball rolling?

Feel free to post as much or as little as you like.

Lectures

To join the lecture, on the Discord server, just click on the Lecture Room voice channel. 

For the best experience for everyone, please ensure your microphone is muted and camera turned off when you join the channel. If at anytime you want to ask a question just unmute yourself and speak up.

If talking out loud isn’t your favourite way of communicating, then you can also just type a message into the #lecture-chat channel.

Online Tutorials (Off-shore Students Only)

Tutorials for off-shore students will also be on Discord, and work mostly the same way as lectures. These will take place in the “Tutorial Main Room”. The tutor will also help students, answer questions, and screenshare with students all in the main room so that everyone can benefit from the answers and feel like they are part of a real tutorial.

To share your screen in the tutorial, simply click the “Screen” button in the bottom left.

 

If the tutor needs to control your screen to help more effectively, they’ll send you a Zoom link.

Discord Channels

Some useful channels have been set up. There are more channels than this, but here are the highlights:

  • #rules – Read these first! It’s mostly common sense, but common sense must be said in this day and age.
  • #announcements – Most MyLO announcements will be mirrored here.
  • #general – Here you can ask general unit-related questions, like about the timetable etc.
  • #assignment-questions– Ask assignment-related questions outside of class here. Staff will do their best to respond here, but feel free to answer each other’s questions too (use common sense to avoid academic misconduct – staff will help guide you on what is acceptable)
  • #off-topic – Talk about random stuff here, but keep it safe-for-work, and respectable.
  • #references-and-resources – If you find somethign you think other students will find useful, then post it here!

    Academic Integrity (Cheating) and Discord

    In almost 5 years of using Discord for teaching, not once has there been an issue with Academic Integrity (within the class servers at least), so let's not start now.

    Use common-sense for what is and isn't appropriate to share. I encourage you to help each other with bugs and problems in your assignments. I even encourage you to share small code snippets (surround your code with the ` symbol to get nice syntax highlighting) of things you would like help with, or to help others.

    In the case of Android projects, you can even share your .apk files (that is a build file, NOT the source code), which others can install on their phones to playtest.

    Obviously, don't post entire files or projects, and also don't post tutorial solutions (or snippets of solutions).

    If I think something is dodgy, I will delete the post. Unless it was a repeat offence or deliberately showing a disregard for the rules, you won't be in trouble, so please lets all make Discord an awesome place for helping each other.
